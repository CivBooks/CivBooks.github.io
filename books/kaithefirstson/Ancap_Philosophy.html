Chapter 2: Anarcho-Capitalism and it's application in Civcraft
Page 9 of 32
Civcraft originally came into existence as Ancap-Minecraft, without any particular goal in mind. The server's open and politically free climate quickly attracted many other persuasions aside from the orginal Ancap inhabitants. The sudden
Page 10 of 32
arrival of several different ideological groups pushed the server's level of civilization and related development forward at a pace that was fascinating. The level of cooperation that this civilization fostered rapidly brought the server to a Utopian
Page 11 of 32
level of server and social development, food was plentiful, building materials were in abundance, and scarcity was unheard of. As one can imagine, this state of affairs was not without it's own set problems; the economy was stagnant, as Capitalism in a
Page 12 of 32
scarcity free environment hasn't been fully worked out. The land became dull and the community was faced with the prospect of becoming stale. To deal with this, Ancap-Minecraft became Civcraft; a grand experiment of communities,politics, discussion, and
Page 13 of 32
debate. Civcraft was from the beginning a different place than Ancap-Minecraft, it was to become more open place and changes were made to make the political dynamics and their ramifications all the more persistent. Ancap philosophy in Civcraft centers
Page 14 of 32
around respect for private property and non-coercion; from these two central rules, many Ancap policies flow. Respect for private property means that what people create and build and own is to be respected and unmolested, and their original intent should
Page 15 of 32
be respected. For example: if someone built a farm that was dependent upon the sunlight, then building a giant pixel art wall after the farm was established that blocked out the sun would infringe upon the farm's integrity and it's intent. Non-coercion
Page 16 of 32
means that transfer of property or services should be voluntary and free from threats of force, for example: if a person wanted property and used force to take seize the property, Ancaps would hold that the property was not acquired legally and the
Page 17 of 32
possession was illegitimate. Ancaps generally believe that monopolies are structurally inclined to drift towards an authoritarian state and inefficient behavior over time. Competition produces new developments whereas Monopolies perpetuate the established
Page 18 of 32
order and often result in the usage of force towards threats to the system of the established order. Monopolies are often inclined toward inefficiency due to their lack of competition, over time without sufficient challenge such a Monopoly becomes
Page 19 of 32
tolerant of self-harming behavior that is not conducive to the original goals of the institution. For example, a police force that has a monopoly on the use of force often is progressively corrupt over time. Ancaps in Civcraft want property to be
Page 20 of 32
respected, and people to be free from coericion in their dealing with one another. Those two tenants are the pillars of Ancap philosophy concerning it's application in Civcraft.

Chapter 3: Property

Central to Anarcho-Capitalism are the concept of self-ownership and the concept of original application. Everyone is the proper owner of his own physical body as well as of all places and nature-given goods that he occupies and puts to use by means of his
Page 23 of 32
body, provided only that no one else has already occupied or used the same places and goods before him. This ownership of "originally appropriated" places and goods by a person implies his right to use and transform these places and goods in any way they
Page 24 of 32
see fit provided only that they do not alter thereby uninvitedly the physical integrity of places and goods originally appropriated by another. In particular, once a place or good has been appropriated first by someone through the infusing one's labor
Page 25 of 32
upon it which is to say that the good can only then be appropriated through voluntary means. Coercion is not considered a legitimate path to acquisition of property.

Chapter 4 Contracts and the Contractual Society.
Page 27 of 32
The society envisioned by anarcho-capitalists has been called the Contractual Society. Ancap Philosophy envisions a society based purely on voluntary action, entirely unhampered by coercion or threats of violence. In which anarcho-capitalists assert the
Page 28 of 32
system is reliant upon voluntary agreements or what is often known as contracts between individuals as the legal framework. The totally voluntary nature of this social order is arguably more free than any other- as any and all obligations are made through
Page 29 of 32
voluntarily contract and agreement, whereas the system of taxation by the state is not. Contracts are the very heart of a functioning social order for Ancap philosophy.
