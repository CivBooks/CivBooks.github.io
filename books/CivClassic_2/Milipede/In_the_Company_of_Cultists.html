§l §rChapter One §l §rThe Dogma of the Ghost in the Machine
Page 7 of 41
The paranormal is a field which I know very little about. This book is not about the paranormal, but about those who believe, those who pursuit, those who honor, those who pray, to Herobrine.
Page 8 of 41
I've come into contact with many such people over the past few years. Some are like-minded scholars investigating the lore of this specter, while others are religious zealots awaiting orders from their divine ruler.
Page 9 of 41
In the early days this scene was full of misinformation and false leads. People such as Copeland claiming to have proof of an apparition, claims of summoning rituals, others even claiming to have "called" Herobrine at 3AM.
Page 10 of 41
Nothing of value was found in these early days. Once interest began to wane we began to see the enthusiasts and the zealots. There where many groups calling themselves §oThe Cult of Harobrine§r, but one stood out among the masses.
Page 11 of 41
§oThe Church of§r §oHerobrine§r. I was unfortunately too late. Abandoned by their leadership, forgotten by their congregation, the ruins of this church was a archive in shambles. I spent weeks piecing together what I could.
Page 12 of 41
With each visit I began to see items that had moved. I was no longer the only church goer. I left a sign asking for any visitor to contact me, and they did. I was contacted by someone going by the name of Clocky.
Page 13 of 41
Clocky was one of the original members of this group. My suspicions were confirmed, this church believed Herobrine to be a "Good Guy". Among many things we disused they also mentioned a book. This book would later become an obsession of mine.
Page 14 of 41
The Nethernomicon. I had seen mentions of this book amidst the mayhem, but I disregarded The Nethernomicon as more misinformation. Clocky was looking for this book so they could return it to their own church.

§oThe Temple of§r §oSaint Herobrine§r, a small but structured organization, was quite a refreshing change of scene from the anarchy that was §oThe Church of§r §oHerobrine§r.

§l §r Chapter Three Stare into the Void, §l §rListen to the Silence
Page 26 of 41
§oThe Listeners§r §oSociety§r, a group of scholars who investigate many different aspects of the world. I spent a considerable amount of time with this group. About two years or so before the group dissolved.
Page 27 of 41
My time with §oThe§r §oListeners Society§r was very enjoyable. This was exactly the environment I was looking for. The investigative lore enthusiast within me flourished here.
Page 28 of 41
We debated the history of music discs, the meaning behind disc 11 and 13, the distant sounds you can hear in deep cavernous mines and tunnels, the influence of gold on the piglins, and the connection between gold mines in the badlands and The Wither.
Page 29 of 41
Among all of these things we conferred on the curiosity that is Herobrine. Although debated, most of us came to the agreed conclusion that Herobrine does exist but does not hold any malicious intent.
Page 30 of 41
Even here I could not escape The Nethernomicon. Although here I was not surrounded by dogmatic diehards, I mostly stayed out of these conversations. The conclusion we reached was that it likely did exist at some point but has been lost to time.
Page 31 of 41
About a years worth of time had passed when we received news that someone by the name of Kminster from a different group had found the location of the first recorded sighting of Herobrine. This brought a few of us back together.
Page 32 of 41

§l §r Chapter Four Ghost Requiem of a Reverie
Page 34 of 41
In a old forgotten chest, inside a long bandoned home, we finally found it. The Nethernomicon was sitting right there, for years untouched. Every description I heard of this book could not do it justice.
Page 35 of 41
I had nearly forgotten about my venture into this strange occult circle. The book I thought couldn't have exited was in the palm of my hands. A shifting skull adorning the cover, morphing text, excerpts from a poem I've never heard but somehow knew.
Page 36 of 41
The tattered pages spoke of various nether-born creatures, staying true to the meaning behind the name, "List of Nether Names". It seams to imply there is some kind of force, or forces, that created these creatures.
Page 37 of 41
Also mentioned in the book is Herobrine. These pages where in the worst condition unfortunately. This author appears to hold secret insight, but frighteningly appears to know very little about who or what Herobrine is or wants.
Page 38 of 41
A sage who knows the answer to so many mysteries and even they cannot crack the enigma that is Herobrine? What hope is there for us to understand this phantom puzzle? I decided to try to find old members of §oThe§r §oTemple of Saint§r §oHerobrine§r.
Page 39 of 41
My search led me to a now dilapidated temple. No doors, no stained glass windows, stripped of all gold decorations, prayers now gone from those brick stone walls. Although I did not necessarily enjoy my time here I still felt incredible grief.
Page 40 of 41
Having learned of the death of such a dedicated community while standing in their most sacred place now desecrated and in ruins was a crushing heartbreak. I left a sign asking for any visitors to contact me. I am still yet to be contacted.
Page 41 of 41
That was almost a year ago now. I still hold with me The Nethernomicon, but I no longer hold much contact with these groups. I fear one day this may all be forgotten, but my biggest fear is that maybe we didn't find the book, maybe the book found us.
