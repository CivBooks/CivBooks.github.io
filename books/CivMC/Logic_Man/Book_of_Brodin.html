1) In the beginning Brodin created the Power Rack and the Olympic Platform. 2) Now the power rack was deserted with an empty barbell, darkness was over the surface of the platform, and the Spirit of Brodin was hovering over the Iron Temple.

3) And Brodin said, “Let there be 45 lb plates,” and there were plates. 4) Brodin saw that the plates were balanced, and he separated the iron plates from the rubber plates.

5) Brodin called the iron “power lifting plates,” and the rubber he called “olympic lifting plates.” And there was reps, and there were sets—the first lifting session.

6) And Brodin said, “Let there be a vault between the reps to separate lifts from lifts.” 7) So Brodin made the vault and separated the reps under the barbell from the weights on it. And it was so.

8) Brodin called the vault “rest.” And there was reps, and there was sets—the second lifting session. 9) And Brodin said, “Let the men under the sky be gathered to one place, and let them pick weights up and put them down repeatedly.” And it was so.

10) Brodin called this movement “deadlifts,” and the gathered men he called “bros.” And Brodin saw that it was good. 11) Then Brodin said, “Let us place heavy-ass weights on a bar and put it on their backs until their quads are so strong
