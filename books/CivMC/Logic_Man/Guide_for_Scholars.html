The teachings of Professor Vexler are manifold and it can take a lifetime before one is able to fully interpret the texts in their most unfiltered form.
Page 4 of 17
This book seeks to teach prospective scholars some key guidelines for ensuring that they are best suited to study the Vexlic Texts.
Page 5 of 17
ON FOOD Meat, in general, should be avoided. The killing of animals is unethical, but sometimes necessary.
Page 6 of 17
Meat consumed purely for pleasure is unethical, though sometimes is necessary for health reasons when such such substitutes are unavailable. A healthy balance of carbohydrates, proteins, fruits and vegetables should be sought.
Page 7 of 17
ON EXERCISE A Vexlic Scholar should exercise regularly. Note that much of the duties of a Vexlic scholar will provide him sufficient work-out for the day, and additional exercise is rarely necessary.
Page 8 of 17
ON HYGIENE It is the duty of every scholar to remain exceedingly hygienic. Regular washing, tooth-brushing and cleaning is necessary.
Page 9 of 17
A scholar’s surroundings must mimic the most pristine of Ape halls and should make the Professor himself proud.
Page 10 of 17
ON SEX Scholars should abstain from sexual intercourse, as it is a distraction from the true pleasures of life, such as understanding the texts.
Page 11 of 17
ON WORK In the Vexlic State, all needs are provided to, and the work of a scholar is that what the Temple demands of him. Beyond the Vexlic state, though, many scholars will find they need an income to support themselves.
Page 12 of 17
Scholars should avoid jobs that would violate parts of the texts like being a stock investor or speculator, and should excel in their work.
Page 13 of 17
Scholars should not work in jobs that would involve anti-Vexlic actions, such as providing supplies to anti-Vexlic forces.
Page 14 of 17
ON BEHAVIOUR Scholars should respect their theological elders and defer to higher authorities on serious matters. Scholars should respect their fellow scholars and fellow man.
Page 15 of 17
ON WAR Vexlic Scholars are warriors at heart, and must embrace war wholeheartedly. Vexlic Scholars should not fear death and must embrace war as a means of learning about the manifold natures of reality.
