Vexlic FAQs This book seeks to answer some key “FAQs” (Frequently Asked Questions) about the Vexlic Creed and the Vexlic Texts. Consult more complex documents for additional information, study the Texts for greater wisdom.
Page 5 of 29
Who is [Ray] Vexler? Professor Ray Vexler PhD is an albino western lowland gorilla, the last of the Great Apes, author of many of the Vexlic Texts, and a being with unparalleled wisdom.
Page 6 of 29
Is Vexler real? Yes.
Page 7 of 29
What are the Vexlic Texts? The Vexlic Texts are a series of texts discovered from the era of Vexler either authored by himself, purported to be authored by him, or concerning him.
Page 8 of 29
Can I read the Vexlic Texts? The full content of the Vexlic Texts are illegible for a normal human. Trained Vexlic Scholars are capable of interpreting the Texts, and it is their duty to spread the knowledge provided.
Page 9 of 29
Where are the Vexlic Texts? That is not public knowledge.
Page 10 of 29
Who/what are the Vexlic Scholars? Anyone who studies the knowledge and wisdom of Professor Vexler can be considered a Vexlic Scholar. The nation-state of Bobanga is governed by Vexlic Scholars,
Page 11 of 29
and is the primary authority in issuing certifications of Scholarhood.
Page 12 of 29
How can I become a Scholar? Read the publicly available induction books for prospective scholars, such as §oGuide for Scholars, Vexlic Cosmology§r and §oBasic Temple Economics.
Page 13 of 29
Is Dar al-Vexler part of Vexler? That question doesn’t make sense. Vexler, aka Ray Vexler, is an Ape Professor who dispenses unlimited wisdom.
Page 14 of 29
Dar al-Vexler is a large city within the country of Bobanga. Dar al-Vexler literally means “Abode of Vexler”, and was named by its founders in honour of the Ape Professor.
Page 15 of 29
What is Bobunga/Bobongo/Bobungo? I don’t know.
Page 16 of 29
What is Bobanga? Bobanga, aka the Vexlic State, is a nation situated within the South-Western part of the continent in a large jungle.
Page 17 of 29
Will there be a second coming of Vexler? No.
Page 18 of 29
Who is Moloch? Moloch is the mortal enemy of Vexler, he is an evil demon who uses cunning, free markets and dark magic to undermine the forces of good in this world.
Page 19 of 29
Moloch is often behind many evils in the world such as the NYSE, Israel, Donald Trump and the country of Burundi.
Page 20 of 29
Why do you hate free markets? Free market capitalism is a tool created by Moloch to undermine nation-states such as Bobanga.
Page 21 of 29
By forcing powerful empires to implement free market reforms, Moloch seeks to undermine them and install his dark rule. Read the Temple Economics series to learn more.
Page 22 of 29
Is Vexler a socialist? No. Socialism involves the rejection of the spiritual teachings of Vexler, and the hierarchical structure of Scholarly rule.
Page 23 of 29
Is Vexler democratic? That question doesn’t make sense.
Page 24 of 29
Is Bobanga democratic? No. A strict autocratic system is required to ensure that the Texts are respected and observed. A democratic system would allow for a powerful demagogue to sway the people away from Vexler.
Page 25 of 29
Is Vexler God? Vexler is not a god, the creator of the universe, omnipotent or even omniscient.
Page 26 of 29
If Vexler is so powerful, why isn’t Bobanga the richest and stronger? Vexler is not an interventionist deity or active wrathful god, but rather a historic figure who has since left us.
Page 27 of 29
He provides us nothing but knowledge and guidance - we still have free will and can fail to meet his expectations.
