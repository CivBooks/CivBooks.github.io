Truths, Book I ------------------- 1. Everything, within the world and without, from the flesh to the soil to spirit itself is contained within the one single point that we call the Universe. 2. The Universe encompasses all because it is all.

3. That which is good and evil alike, which is light and dark, warm and cold, animate and lifeless, is all once, for they are all the Universe. 4. As the Universe is the divine totality of all things, anything born out of the Universe is imperfect, for

it is only a fraction of that which contains all. 5. From the Universe came that which we call the Architect of the World. Being only a fraction, the Architect could not possibly make use of the Wisdom present in the Universe. 6. In its ignorance, the

known as the All-Spirit. 3. The All-Spirit is wisdom, mercy, and justice. The essence of the All-Spirit is the essence of the soul. 4. Pitying the creatures of the world, the All-Spirit breathed fragments of soul into the inhabitants of the world.

5. The senseless automatons created by the Architect were filled with the wisdom and energy of the All-Spirit, and were able to develop as conscious and moral beings. 6. Animals were given smaller fragments of soul, and humans were given larger souls.

The Righteous Path ------------------- 1. This is the speech that Ostracod gave when he enlightened the people of Yoslan: 2. The essence of the Body is Matter, and tends towards serving its own worldly needs. 3. The essence of Soul is Wisdom.
Page 26 of 50
4. The Soul being made of Wisdom, is what makes people capable of kindness, love, and learning. 5. The ensoulment of humanity by the Spirit of Wisdom was the first and last act of Kindness in the universe. For without her Wisdom, we would be living dead.
Page 27 of 50
6. The only agents in the Universe capable of Wise acts are people, for animals have lesser souls than humans and unliving things have no souls at all. 7. A person is Soul and Body, while a fungus is entirely Body.
Page 28 of 50
A person is capable of good and evil while a fungus is mindless and can only act out the basic will of its Body. 8. I have not come as a lawgiver, but to lay down the principles of a morality based on the Soul. I give you the principles of Wisdom thusly:
Page 29 of 50
9. A good person is someone who does what a soulless creature would be incapable of doing. 10. A soulless creature would hold no obligations to its clan or its community. This act we shall not follow.
Page 30 of 50
11. A soulless creature would refuse to aid the poor and weak. This act we shall not follow. 12. A soulless creature would strive singularly to avoid bodily death. This act we shall not follow. 13. A soulless creature would tread upon its
Page 31 of 50
allies and friends as soon as they cease to be useful. This act we shall not follow. 14. A soulless creature would meet its own bodily desires before it seeks to meet its spiritual desires. This act we shall not follow.
Page 32 of 50
15. A soulless creature would seek only the domination of others and of nature. This act we shall not follow. 16. A soulless creature would put wealth and power above honor and duty. This act we shall not follow.

Ostracod Dialogue ------------------- 1. This is an account of the Dialogue between Ostracod the Enlightened one and the King of the Westlanders. 2. The King pointed his finger at Ostracod and accused him: "There shall be no cults in Norlund!"

3. Ostracod responded: "How could it be a cult that offers the Universal Truth?" 4. The King laughed and said, "Is it not every cult that claims to have the universal truth?" 5. "You are mistaken," said Ostracod,

Apotheosis ------------------- 1. Ostracod had reached the age of 55 when he spoke to the Yoslanders again: 2. "I have imparted unto you my Nonniq Gos [Spiritual Knowledge]. Go forth and expand on it." 3. Then his Body died and his Soul joined Wisdom.

Temple Building ------------------- [The following is not treated as scripture, but rather is a set of notes on Marquis-era temple construction.] 1. As the body is mortal, churches are set into the ground so that the floor is below the land level.
Page 47 of 50
2. As the soul is free, churches have no windows or doors, and wind is permitted to flow into the building. 3. As the desires of the body are base, churches are to be sparsely decorated. 4. As prayer is private, churches are to fit a half dozen at most.
Page 48 of 50
5. As the needs of the Soul are imminent, churches should be spread across all populated areas. 6. As even a simple building can be a luxury, worshippers are permitted to pray in a ditch if a church is not avaliable.
Page 49 of 50
7. As neither the Soul, nor Wisdom, nor any prophet, nor the Universe can be represented with any one symbol, churches are to be free of idols, icons, and symbols. 8. As prayer is a sacred activity, churches are sacred buildings.
