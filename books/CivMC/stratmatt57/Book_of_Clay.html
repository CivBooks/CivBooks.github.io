Polemic I: The Origin Wandering throughout the lands beyond our world, our Lord Etahn found a beautiful land. He watched this place from afar, observing its people go about their lives. Toiling the land, waiting long hours
Page 3 of 25
hoping their crops may grow in order to see another day. Etahn pitied their way of life. Putting in such great effort for a reward they may never see, due to the threat of conflict and war these people faced constantly.
Page 4 of 25
How could they tolerate living this way? Despite this, He admired these people for their will to create and their perseverance even when faced with the most trying of circumstances. Etahn wished to help them.

Polemic II: The Gift One day, Etahn began to see great swathes of land disappear. He found these people He admired gathering, awaiting the end of their world.
Page 6 of 25
Despite the end of this world coming, Etahn knew a fresh world would take its place, and that these people would return with it. And so the world ended, and as Etahn knew, a new one took its place.
Page 7 of 25
These people once again began to return to their previous lives. Building cities anew, toiling their land and facing the hardships they had before. Etahn saw a man sitting beside a pool atop a hill. He went to him and talked to this man.
Page 8 of 25
The man taught Him of their world and pleaded Etahn to help. Etahn told the man He would imbue his essence into what these people knew as Clay.
Page 9 of 25
He said this Holy material would bless the land with life and fertility, helping the land and its people prosper. He said all those in this land would recieve His Gift, but those that followed His teachings and worshipped Him would be blessed
Page 10 of 25
...and those that deserved His wroth would be cursed. Our Lord dipped His hand into the pool. Pillars of Clay rose to the sky as the pool poured out His Holy waters, to flow their way throughout the world to spread His blessing.

And with that, the world was forever changed.

Polemic III: The Claymandments I: Accept me as your God, giver of this gift that has blessed your land. Worship me above all else. II: Do not desecrate that which is Holy.
Page 13 of 25
III: Words are wind, those that truly wish to prove their devotion to me must commit themselves to the Clayjj Pilgrimage. Journey to this place where I have given My gift unto your lands.
