Prawny's Meditation Book CivMC can be a wonderful experience where players enjoy the company of friends and the satisfaction of large-scale projects coming to completion. However, CivMC can also be a stressful,

at times even painful, experience. During difficult situations, it's more important than ever to look after your mental health and take some time to calm your mind. To help you in this endeavor, I will walk you through a common breathing exercise.

To begin, find a comfortable place to sit or lie down. Focus your mind on a peaceful location, a place that feels safe and quiet. Imagine a high mountain peak, perhaps, or a boundless ocean shore where the waves roll in and out.
Page 4 of 15
Bring your awarness to your breathing, focusing your full attention to the way your chest rises and falls with every breath. Take a deep breath through your nose, letting your lungs expand as far as they can.
Page 5 of 15
Hold that breath for 5-7 seconds, slowly counting out the time in your mind. One. Two. Three. Four. Five. Then, slowly exhale for 8 seconds, mentally counting out the time. One. Two. Three. Four. Five. Six. Seven. Eight.
Page 6 of 15
Continue to inhale, hold your breath, and exhale in the same way, focusing on each breath. Let your thoughts wander away from the things you are worried about: lingering anxiousness over the past or concerns of the future.
Page 7 of 15
For this exercise, focus only on the present moment. Keep your mind in that safe place, perhaps with the aid of some soft, relaxing music. If it's difficult to stay focused, keep your mind fixed on counting, thinking only about the tension in your chest
Page 8 of 15
as you inhale and the relaxing of your muscles as you exhale. You can also use mental mantras to help focus your mind and add positivity to your thoughts. As you inhale, mentally say, "inhaling peace and calm."

Then, as you exhale, mentally say, "exhaling tension and anxiety." This type of exercise is called resonant or coherent breathing, and it only takes a few minutes to have a profound effect. It's often used to help with bouts of anxiety throughout the day
Page 10 of 15
and can also help increase cognitive function. By taking a step back and allowing your mind to calm, you can clear your head and improve your focus for the difficult and complex tasks you perform every day. Try this exercise for

about five minutes and see how you feel. Planning just a little time every day to step back from a project and set yourself firmly in the present moment can make a meaningful difference. A great mentality to have when practicing this form of meditation
