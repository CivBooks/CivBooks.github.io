§lThe Braemari Peopl§re§r The Braemari trace their origins to Salisbury, a verdant region in Gabon. Here, they cultivated mushrooms, a vital crop. Their success was overshadowed by unrest, as society grew disdainful of this reliance, sparking a revolt.
Page 3 of 30
The cultural upheaval led to the persecution of the Braemari people, forcing them to flee their homeland. Seeking safety, they embarked on a perilous journey to lands unknown, leaving behind their ancestral soil.
Page 4 of 30
After arduous travel, the Braemari settled in the untamed landscapes of what is now Pavia. They turned to wool farming, using sheep for sustenance and trade. Their relationship with the land was one of harmony and survival.
Page 5 of 30
Living near the sea, they became skilled fishermen and adept navigators. The ocean became both a source of livelihood and a spiritual element in their lives, symbolizing resilience and exploration.
Page 6 of 30
Braemari society was structured under the leadership of Cissonius I, the First Lord of the Admiralty. As their leader, he provided unity and stability, ensuring their survival in these new and challenging lands.
Page 7 of 30
The Braemari developed a culture of self-sufficiency and adaptability. They valued internal cohesion over external alliances, remaining insular and cautious of outsiders to preserve their way of life.
Page 8 of 30
§lWinds of Change: The Arrival of Pavia§r The arrival of the Pavian settlers heralded a new era for the Braemari. Led by Crown Prince BritishWanderer I, the Pavians brought visions of prosperity and a structured society to the region.
Page 9 of 30
A historic meeting between Crown Prince BritishWanderer I and First Lord of the Admiralty Cissonius I marked the beginning of relations. Gifts were exchanged, symbolizing cooperation and mutual respect.
Page 10 of 30
The Pavians rapidly built a trade city, attracting the Braemari with opportunities in farming, politics, and the military. Initially hesitant, the Braemari began integrating into the burgeoning Pavian society.
Page 11 of 30
Braemar was officially incorporated as a Duchy under Pavia. Cissonius I became Duke of Braemar, ensuring the continued representation of his people in Pavian governance while embracing this prosperous new chapter.
Page 12 of 30
The Braemari faced challenges integrating culturally, as they dispersed across the city rather than forming a cultural quarter. Over time, they blended their heritage with Pavian customs.
Page 13 of 30
This balance of tradition and assimilation transformed Pavia into a shared home for the Braemari, allowing them to preserve their identity while embracing the opportunities of their new nation.
Page 14 of 30
The Elysian Pact, comprising Pavia, Volterra, Estalia, and SPQR, aimed to maintain stability. However, tensions between Estalia and Rhode Island escalated, drawing the region into the Generic War.
Page 15 of 30
On 26 July, the excavation of a bunker, later named Terminus, was ordered to safeguard Pavia. Its strategic location near the train station made it a critical defensive hub during the war.
Page 16 of 30
The Coast Guard introduced a rigorous training regimen, the BritishWanderer Baseline, under Cissonius's command. Recruits were tested against the Crown Prince, ensuring readiness for combat.
Page 17 of 30
Rhode Island forces bombarded Pavia, leaving pillars of obsidian across the landscape. Cissonius’s leadership in repelling these attacks solidified his reputation as a skilled and strategic leader.
Page 18 of 30
During one ambush, Cissonius, while clearing debris, outmanoeuvred four Rhode Island fighters, leading them into a trap where allied forces from Pavia and Estalia countered the attack.
Page 19 of 30
The Braemari people’s resilience and strategic contributions helped secure victory. The defeat of Rhode Island on 17 September 2022 marked a turning point in Pavian history, with Braemar playing a crucial role.
Page 20 of 30
§lTrials of Settlement: The Braemari Migration§r Despite their integration into Pavia, the Braemari struggled to find a permanent home within the city. Plots were offered, yet none resonated with their deep connection to land and heritage.
Page 21 of 30
This search for a home became known as the Braemari Migration, a defining chapter in their history. It symbolized their yearning for a place where their traditions could flourish once more.
Page 22 of 30
Ultimately, the Braemari returned to Kilmoran, their ancestral island. Though in ruins, the island’s cultural significance inspired a determined effort to restore it to its former glory.
Page 23 of 30
Under Cissonius’s leadership, Kilmoran was revitalized. Infrastructure was rebuilt, and the capital building, a symbol of Braemari life, was restored to serve as a hub for their community.
Page 24 of 30
The building featured cold storage for meat and wool, a community hall for gatherings, and accommodations for travellers. It became a sanctuary where Braemari traditions were preserved.
Page 25 of 30
The Braemari Migration concluded with triumph as Kilmoran became a beacon of resilience and heritage, blending modern functionality with deep reverence for their cultural roots.
Page 26 of 30
§lBraemari Luminaries: Leaders of Pavia§r Following Gobblin II’s death, Cissonius was elected Duke and later Emperor. His reign saw pivotal events, including the Purple War, where Pavia triumphed over Kallos, solidifying its power.
Page 27 of 30
Kilmoran served as the staging ground for the campaign, with troops and supplies departing from its shores. The swift victory dismantled Kallosian infrastructure and captured their fighters.
Page 28 of 30
The term "Rat King" was given to Cissonius to honour the Braemari miners. Their underground work on bastions was critical to Pavian security, symbolizing their dedication to this nation they call home.
Page 29 of 30
Cissonius’s death marked the end of an era. Tributes poured in from across the world, celebrating his life and leadership. His legacy endured through his successors, Cissonius II and III.
Page 30 of 30
The Braemari people remain integral to Pavia’s history, their resilience and contributions woven into the fabric of a nation they helped build, defend, and guide into a prosperous future.
