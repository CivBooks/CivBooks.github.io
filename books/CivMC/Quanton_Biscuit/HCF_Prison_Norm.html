~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ 3. WorldPopo Violence and Imprisonment Norms ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ This chapter alone would constitute many books. History on this topic would absorb many volumes, and already there exists a wealth on Google documents and Reddit
Page 21 of 48
sources documenting cases of injustice on the part of the WorldPopo power structure. This section will briefly describe the structure of the WorldPopo imposed force structure, and the norms of violence and imprisonment it established. These will
Page 22 of 48
prove as instrumental in producing HCF's own indefinite imprisonment policy. ------ The WorldPolice sanctioned various players in its history with permanent (or indefinite) imprisonment. These players fall into a few broad categories:
Page 23 of 48

Page 24 of 48
large-scale thiefs. For examples, search the reddit for "pearling" threads. Examples abound. (2) Prisoners who owed a "doable" amount of damages, but who had no reasonable way to produce payment. Players in this class simply owed
Page 25 of 48
too much; they couldn't feasibly play Minecraft for dozens of hours a week just to release themselves from imaginary prison, and so they were effectively permanently imprisoned.
Page 26 of 48
(3) Presumed recidivists. Many cases, the adjudicators simply judged that the criminal could not and would not reform. This subjective judgement was used as excuse to keep the person imprisoned,

regardless of the nature or severity of the offense.

(4) Players who were totally unknown. Many players kept by the WorldPolice were kept without reason, as nobody holding the pearls could remember why they were held. Thus, they were held because "it would be too risky to release them". This GITMO-
Page 29 of 48

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ Rational Response to WorldPopo Violence Norms ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ When considering how to approach an enemy, one should consider how that enemy might react. Often, examining rational response is a good first approximation.
Page 34 of 48
While people fall far short of rationality, people who are capable of even a *modicum* of analysis and careful thought will settle on, more or less, rational solutions. Regardless, it's important to investigate the rational response to the threats of
Page 35 of 48
violence that was presented by the WorldPolice during the HCF invasion. Despite the clear irrationality of certain members of the HCF, it seems clear that the group as a whole would be more likely to stumble upon a clear-headed and rational response.

What changed? HCF members were wise to the reddit. They saw that WorldPopo members habitually imprisoned people indefinitely.

They saw that they were under threat of being imprisoned indefinitely. Given that this was a possibility (rather than the norms they were used to on HCF), the game changed immediately.
Page 43 of 48
Now there was a non-zero probability that their players would be *taken out of the occasion*. Thus, if they continued to release their enemies (or potential enemies) they simply created a possibility of being beaten forever.

This explains the indefinite imprisonment and the growing paranoia that even the innocent members they held would become violent. Knowing that they had made mistakes, they began
Page 45 of 48
to feel exposed by the possibilities they knew were lurking in the shadows. They could release the WorldPopo or others, of course, but they *knew* they would be risking permanent
Page 46 of 48

Given repeated play and enough repetitions, dynamic systems end up in "absorbing states" or states where things cease to change. The HCF knew that continually freeing their competition meant risking getting themselves permanently pearled.
