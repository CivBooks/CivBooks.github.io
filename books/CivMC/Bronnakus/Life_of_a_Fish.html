A look into the Life, Times and History Of JesstheFish or, an autobiography written by JesstheFish Transcribed by Bronnakus

The life of JesstheFish, while not a long tale, is an interesting one. It begins in October of 2019, when she first spawned in the CivRealms server. She explored for a while, read her starter book, and rather quickly learned she could shout so loud that the whole world
Page 3 of 17
would hear her. Using this newfound ability, she spoke her first words: "hello". Aresot, the King of a burgeoning Varathia, heard her should and extended to her an offer of citizenship in his empire. She quickly accepted, and there built the first iteration of a recurring
Page 4 of 17
building - the tavern known as the Maenad Tavern. It was a simple building - in fact, she didn't even realise she could actually brew alcohol until after the build was complete. But once she learned how alcohol worked, she was infatuated with it. She sought membership with the
Page 5 of 17
Varathian Brewers Guild, headed by TTastic and Albyrt, and was given a quest - if she could brew a beer, a wine, and a liquor, she would be granted apprenticeship under the Guild. She set to work immediately. She brewed first lager,
Page 6 of 17
then a nice pisco. While it would take longer, she created vodka as well. A week later, in November, she grew bored with life in Varathia. It was a nice life, but dull. She grew tired of even the idea of the Empier, and sought a new system, a system which as yet

did not exist. So, she sailed off, and settled the site that would become La Commune d'Athena, beginning with the Fort Eve, on November 6, with some help from ICOR. Cabo and esorscher would later join her, and the three of them would build La Commune into a nice settlement. However,
Page 8 of 17
all good things must end, and after a month that grew plagued with problems, so too did the Commune. So, where was Jess to go? She couldn't return to Varathia. However, when esorcher left, he offered her something good - citizenship in

Wheatistan. This was something she considered, and an offer accepted by her. After a vote to grant Jess citizenship, she moved to Wheaton and set up a second version of the Maenad. This version was an open-air bar, and while it produced a good amount of alcohol, never sold

any. After a confusing week, she left Wheaton for the city of Alexandria. Alexandria was itself a new city - it was only a couple weeks ago it had been founded, but it had expanded rapidly. It was the very day that Alexandria declared its independence that
