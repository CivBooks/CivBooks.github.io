Article 3 Judicial Power Section 1: Highest judicial power in the Empire of Columbia shall fall upon the Supreme Judge of Columbia. The Supreme Judge of Columbia shall be responsible for overseeing cases of
Page 2 of 14
civil and criminal law, determining the innocence or guilt of the prosecuted, and prescribing just punishment in accordance with the Criminal and Penal Codes of Columbia. The Supreme Judge of Columbia must abide by a defined procedure for handling court cases, agreed upon
Page 3 of 14
by the Supreme Judge and Emperor of Columbia. Section 2: The Supreme Judgedom of the Empire of Columbia is an elected office, thus it is subject to the aspiration and determination of the Full Citizens of Columbia. Terms shall
Page 4 of 14
be the medium through which an elected individual serves as the Supreme Judge of Columbia. A term shall be defined as a period of four months. Section 3: No limit shall be established regarding the number of terms which an elected Supreme Judge may
Page 5 of 14
serve; conversely, no alteration of legality may be made which would inhibit the election cycle of the Supreme Judgedom, to the end of an individual maintaining power beyond the elected terms. Section 4: Elections for the Supreme Judge of
Page 6 of 14
the Empire of Columbia shall be held normally, once every fourth months. If an elected Supreme Judge is to resign their position ahead of the mandated time of termination, a new Judge must be appointed by the President of Columbia to serve out the remainder of their
Page 7 of 14
term until the normal election cycle begins. Section 5: Candidates for the Supreme Judgedom of Columbia must declare their bid a minimum of forty eight hours before the polling period begins for an election. The vote of the Full Citizens of Columbia shall then
Page 8 of 14
be cast in a period of twenty four hours before the official termination of the sitting Supreme Judge, and will be announced to the general population by the Emperor at the time of termination. Section 6: The Ejection, that being the removal
Page 9 of 14
from office, of a Supreme Judge of Columbia may be brought about by a formal Call for Ejection from the President of Columbia. For a Call for Ejection to be ratified, a seven tenths majority vote amongst the Full Citizenry is required. If a Call for Ejection
Page 10 of 14
of a sitting Supreme Judge is ratified, a new Supreme Judge must be appointed by the Emperor of Columbia to serve out the remaineder of their term. Section 7: The Supreme Judge of Columbia may appoint lower Arbiters for local or less
Page 11 of 14
significant court cases at their discretion. If a party to an Arbiter's case believes their ruling to be in legal error, the case may be appealed to the Supreme Judge, who shall deliver a final ruling. Section 8: If a court case
Page 12 of 14
involves the Supreme Judge of Columbia as one of the disputing parties, the Emperor of Columbia shall be responsible for appointing an Arbiter to rule on the case. Section 9: As an autonomous region of the Empire of Columbia, the Boule of the Realm of
Page 13 of 14
Valyria may appoint their own High Judge of Valyria to handle local cases of law in accordance with the Charter of Valyria. The Supreme Judge of Columbia shall hold higher authority than the High Judge of Valyria in cases being requested for appeal, and shall serve as the primary judicial
Page 14 of 14
authority in Valyria if the Boule does not appointed their own High Judge of Valyria. End Section Three.
