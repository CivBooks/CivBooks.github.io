There are structural problems with Discord. Both in the metagame and for civilizations growth in game. §0 §0 §0Meta gamers are a cancer on the civ genre, but thats a topic for another book. Discord does not only encourage metagaming, but
Page 2 of 13
it practically forces players to become metagamers. With the phone app, a website and pushed notifications, every player with discord is constantly connected to the other players in your nation. There is no more "Unavailable" for any player.

Further, discord also often hosts entire communication networks between nations, so now someone who only plays once a week can have tangible effects on communications and relationships between nations. Even intrastate policy and elections can be run
Page 4 of 13
on discord, which completely removes the obligation to play the game in order to have an effect on policy. §0 §0 §0Second, Discord is a seriously broken app or website. The app constantly crashes in the middle of VC and oftentimes messages get double sent.

This doesnt include the numerous times images dont load properly on the app or website or when discord is down region or nationwide. §0 §0 §0This also doesnt bode well for civilizations in game for growth. Early civilizations often use discord to facilitate

easier to be a metagamer and talk on discord than it is to play the game and communicate thusly. §0 §0 §0Finally, discord is a heaven send to authoritarians. You see, discord has the ability to allow the server owner to mute or ban certain people from your

So what's the answer? What does a nation do? Well, first, its important to decentralize the means of communication. Use discord, but also use sykpe, mumble, even in game chat at times. Prioritize DM's and use subreddits for announcements. Also, its

important to sometimes disconnect. Dont download the app and at the very least turn off push notifications. Real life comes first.
