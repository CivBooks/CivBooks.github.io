Preamble We the Citizens of Anrodia in the Name of Almighty God do write and affirm this, the Constitution of Anrodia, which shall provide all basic and fundamental rights to all citizens, these being life, liberty, happiness, and property. It shall also

restore the proud heritage of our land, and declare to all a united Anrodia. Article I: Government There shall be three branches of Government, these being the Executive, Judicial, and Legislative. They shall be completely separate from each

other, except in cases where the safety of the Citizens requires the intermingling of branches. These three branches shall exist in all provinces/states. Section I: Executive The Executive Branch shall consist of a President, a Vice President, a Cabinet
Page 4 of 23
appointed by the President, and other Executive staff, these being elected by all Citizens (the President and Vice President), and appointed by the President (the President’s Cabinet and other staff). The President is the de facto head of state, with the Vice President being the reserve
Page 5 of 23
head of state in case of the President’s incapacity. They shall be under the Rule of Law, and shall never be allowed to thwart it. The President’s power may be overruled by the Judicial Branch, and/or the People. Lastly, the President holds vetoing power for all bills; however, this vetoing power
Page 6 of 23
must not be used excessively, as using it excessively would impede democracy. Section II: Legislative The Legislative Branch shall consist of one house, the National Council. This house shall consist of one elected member from each province of Anrodia, and shall
Page 7 of 23
only exist to draft a piece of legislature, a bill, act, or other document, and to send it to the Citizens, the true legislature, for a national referendum. If the Citizens approve by a simple majority for smaller actions or a two-thirds majority for larger actions, the bill shall be sent
Page 8 of 23
to the President for a final signing into law . Section III: Judicia The Judicial Branch shall consist of a Supreme Court of Anrodia. This Court shall be the highest court in the nation, and shall exist to determine whether a bill/law is unconstitutional, to
Page 9 of 23
serve appeals of national importance, and to hear cases of national importance. The Court shall hold its own discretion on whether to hear a case. This Court shall also be completely independent from other forms of government, and it shall also be under the Rule of Law. There
Page 10 of 23
shall be an odd number of justices, ranging from a minimum of three to a maximum of fifteen. Justices are chosen by the President, and must be confirmed by both a two-thirds majority in the National Council and a referendum issued to the Citizens. Lastly, there shall be lower district courts,

their members are appointed by the President, and follow the same process of appointment as Supreme Court Justices.

Section IV: Salary All members of the Government must receive a salary, this is decided by the Citizens and the National Council in a joint session.

Article II: Elections All elections must be held once every four years, starting in the year of our Lord two thousand and twenty-two. There shall be no primaries, only one singular election on the date, which shall be the first Monday of November. Section I: Executive
Page 14 of 23
The President shall start their campaign one year before the election date, and shall appoint their Vice President (running mate) within that time. There will be a two-term limit for the President, with one term being four years. To become President, one must be a naturalized citizen

of Anrodia, thirty years old, and have no felonies or heinous crimes committed. Section II: Legislative The same process for executive elections shall be for the legislative; however, all elections are every two years, one on the same election date as the

Presidential/General Election, and one two years after, this being the midterm. There shall also be a two-term limit for each member of the National Council; however, one term is two years instead of four. One must be twenty-five years of age, a citizen of Anrodia for five years, and a
Page 17 of 23
citizen of their representing state for one year to become a National Councilman. Section III: Judicial As stated in Article I, Section III, there will be no elections for any member of any of Anrodia’s Courts, as they are all appointed by the President.

Article III: Powers of the Legislature The National Legislature has powers devoted to itself; this article shall outline them without specificity.

Section I: Economy The National Council sets all laws regarding taxes, debts, and the national budget. This shall be done in a singular session of Council, with the outcome being decided by a two-thirds majority vote by the Council and Citizens.

Section II: Other Powers The Council is also responsible for maintaining the general welfare of all, the national militia, all national roads, post offices, outlining national punishment for felonies (federal crimes), and other needs that need
