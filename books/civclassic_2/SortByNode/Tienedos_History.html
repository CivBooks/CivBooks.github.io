Tienedo:§0 §0A History Written by President E101K of Tienedo.§0 §0 §0Long ago, before the advent of civilized society, there was an ancient string of tribes that were populated by the Tienedese people.

They strived to live§0 §0in peace and in equilibrium with§0 §0the nature and environment they lived inside, but also wished to develop and innovate. This desire for advancement led to the Tienedese founding a Republic in their jungles,

located in the south of Civexia Island in the Bay of Tienedo that sought to not only provide a civilized society for the ex-tribesmen, women and children, but also to carry on the tradition of Tegngat, which means "Holy Forest" in the Tienedese language.

This "religion" of soughts was the heart of the Old Republic, and the strong agricultural development and trade with other states such as the Survival Union and Kuren were the muscle and backbone of the Republic.

Page 8 of 15
Tienedo. Those who originally lived there were cast out to surrounding states and areas, or worse, sentenced to the mad life of the past, that is: living in an uncivilized tribe that pillaged towns and robbed villagers.
Page 9 of 15
The people of Tienedo had lost hope, and for 300 years they lived outside of the one place they longed for: their home with the grand jungles and lush coastlines.
Page 10 of 15
Descentants of the old Tienedese Republic decided it was time to revive the long lost region, and rebuild the 90% of buildings that were destroyed during the conflicts and poverty.
Page 11 of 15
Eventually, after hard work and faith in their nation, the New Republic of Tienedo was founded on top of the ruins of the Old Republic of Tienedo with its capital proclaimed in Tienedo Town.

The once deserted, broken wasteland had been transformed into a new, brave nation that sought to carry on the traditions of the old people of Tienedo, revive the sleeping Republic of Tienedo and return to a time of not only harmony with nature,
