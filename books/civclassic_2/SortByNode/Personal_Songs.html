There is no path for this lonely walker to follow, yet they seem to know exactly where they are going. In fact, they even strode with a gait that only belongs to one with a purpose.

Everyone knows the peaceful sound of snow crunching under boots. If you have ever experienced it, it's still easy to imagine that crisp yet soft sound.
Page 9 of 40
It's a peaceful and consistent sound, one that keeps the beat of the world, much like the way a drum softly beats in the background of chants or plays. Crunching leaves has a similar sound, each one the drumbeat of their season.
Page 10 of 40
Sometimes, when in the right place and right time, leaves will blanket the snow, which has blanketed the ground, and the sounds combine, producing a natural harmony.
Page 11 of 40
It happens often while walking through woods, though mostly scattered and unpredictable, like a freeform indie song. It's calming to hear the way the brittle leaves crinkle and the snow crunch.§0

When walking, people often have a gait, a certain way that they space their footsteps and create the rhythm to their own personal song. Some are fast paced, like that of a business man. Others are slow and steady, much like that of gardener.
Page 13 of 40
That pace helps set their mood, their cadence of life. Sometimes that pace is accented by another beat, an elderly man and his cane, a boy and his dog or even two lovers whose cadence is intertwined.
Page 14 of 40
A person's cadence changes with events in their life, sometimes it changes often, depending on the mood and how easily their emotions influence them, some change rarely and stay relatively consistent.
Page 15 of 40
No matter how often it changes, the tempo of their pace, or if it is accentuated or not, that rhythm is personal and unique.§0

Tone is important to the way a song is taken in. Tone is that underlying feel to a song, that business man walking on concrete might be a rushed and dramatic tone filled with anxious pauses,
Page 17 of 40
while the gardener's might be a slow and serene feel while he walks through the mossy greenhouse. If you could hear the personal song of a person, or even your own, that would be a staple to their personality and their inner mind.
Page 18 of 40
Much like the tempo, tone changes often, but unlike tempo when the song is heard from beginning to end, there is always an overtone, a grand feeling to the whole piece.
Page 19 of 40
If you could hear your song would you find the tone to have a happy one or something more melancholy? What if you only heard the song as it has been within the last week? Or month? Or year? Would the changes be significant?
Page 20 of 40
Makes you wonder doesn't it, how much the way you walk and the way you carry that walk can convey your feelings and inner thoughts? Perhaps it is sad that we cannot hear these songs.
Page 21 of 40
When you think of a song one does not remember the bass line or the drum beat, unless that is the focus of the song, mostly you remember the melody and the lyrics, if they have any.
Page 22 of 40
Typing, clicking your mouth, spoken words, even sneezing and coughing, are the melodies of one's personal song. Occasionally choruses appear, a repeating section that comes and goes in between the randomness of verses.
Page 23 of 40
So while we all know what the sound of crunching snow under a boot sounds like, we all remember the words and small mannerisms that a person makes when we get closer to knowing their personal song.
Page 24 of 40
We start remembering others' melodies as we start identifying a person. When you remember a song, you find you might remember only specific parts, like the chorus, or a specific verse or two, much like you can easily recall certain things about a person,
Page 25 of 40
their favorite color, the way their words flow, or even the facial expression when they get mad. The melody is in the forefront of a song, the desired part of a song that sticks in a person's mind.§0

When a songwriter, or writers in general, create a piece they always know that a large part of the way it is received comes down to personal interpretation.
